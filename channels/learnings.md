- [2026-06-16 · craft · video generation] A generated clip must be ONE continuous take, faithful to its keyframe — never a multi-cut montage. Hard cuts / "snap-cut" / multiple BEATS inside a single ~8s clip make the video model re-roll each cut as a fresh scene: it loses continuity, duplicates characters and invents new ones, and contradicts the still (e.g. a person on a gurney shown running). Write clip briefs as one camera move + one continuous action, featuring ONLY the people/props in that keyframe (no extra background figures — crowds, paramedics, bystanders — they render inconsistently and duplicate the mains), with every action physically consistent with the frame's staging.
- [2026-06-16 · craft · staging] When a keyframe freezes a two-person hand-off of a prop, the still is DIRECTION-AMBIGUOUS and the video model often animates the exchange BACKWARDS. Either stage the giver unmistakably (who reaches, who receives) or avoid the live hand-off entirely — have the prop already settled in one character's hand.

- Spend available budget on dynamic, animated clips with real motion rather than static keyframes/poses.
- Choreograph each clip as a sequence of beats (reach → grab → act → result) so the action reads as a mini-story instead of a single frozen pose.
- Keep shot compositions simple and easy to generate — when a shot reads as "weird," rework it into a clear, shootable one rather than pushing a convoluted frame.
- Establish the before-state before any transformation (object intact before the cut, grasped correctly by its handle) so cause visibly precedes effect and the action is legible.
