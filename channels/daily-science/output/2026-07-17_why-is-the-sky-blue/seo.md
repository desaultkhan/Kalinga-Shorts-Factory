TITLE
Why Is The Sky Blue And Not Violet? The Real Answer

DESCRIPTION
Why is the sky blue and not violet when violet light scatters more in the atmosphere? Violet light DOES scatter more than blue through air molecules. But our eyes are far more sensitive to blue wavelengths than violet, and the upper atmosphere absorbs a chunk of that violet before it even reaches us. So instead of a purple sky, we see brilliant blue. Follow for more surprising science every day.
