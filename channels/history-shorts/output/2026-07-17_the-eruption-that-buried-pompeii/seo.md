TITLE
Pompeii's Last Second: How Victims Really Died

DESCRIPTION
How did Pompeii victims die in 79 AD? Not from suffocation like most think. When Mount Vesuvius erupted, a pyroclastic surge of superheated gas vaporized blood and boiled brains in less than a second. Some skulls exploded from internal pressure. It was instant, not the slow death we imagined. Follow for more history moments.
